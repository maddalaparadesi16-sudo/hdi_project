Analytical Methodology

How is the Official HDI Calculated?

The HDI is a geometric mean of normalized indices for each of the three dimensions:

Dimension Indices

1. Health Index (LEI): \(\text{LEI} = \frac{\text{Life Expectancy} - 20}{85 - 20}\)

2. Education Index (EI): \(\text{EI} = \frac{\text{Expected Schooling Index} + \text{Mean Schooling Index}}{2}\)

  • \(\text{Expected Schooling Index (EYSI)} = \frac{\text{EYS}}{18}\)
  • \(\text{Mean Schooling Index (MYSI)} = \frac{\text{MYS}}{15}\)

3. Income Index (II): \(\text{II} = \frac{\ln(\text{GNI per capita}) - \ln(100)}{\ln(75,000) - \ln(100)}\)

Composite Index

\(HDI = \sqrt[3]{\text{LEI} \times \text{EI} \times \text{II}}\)

Machine Learning Model Specs

Our application leverages a Linear Regression model trained on socioeconomic inputs to approximate the composite scoring. By fitting the model on global data, it learns the coefficients representing how changes in health, education, and income contribute to human development ratings.

Feature Indicator Importance Coefficient Interpretation
Mean Years of Schooling 0.01766 Reflects current educational achievement of the adult population.
Expected Years of Schooling 0.01397 Reflects educational access and expectation for the younger generation.
Life Expectancy 0.00725 Measures longevity, healthcare infrastructure, and public health conditions.
GNI per Capita 0.000001 Measures standard of living. Note that this feature is heavily weighted by scale, hence standard regression coefficients appear smaller, yet GNI contributes significantly to the final classification.
